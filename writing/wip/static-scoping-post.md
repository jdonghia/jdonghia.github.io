Do you know how scopes work in JavaScript?

With AI, we're adding more layers of abstraction to programming knowledge than ever, and fundamental concepts like scopes are something a
lot of developers might skip over.

I’ve been spending the last few months going back to basics and documenting my studies on JavaScript fundamentals. So, I just published a
new post on Scoping, sharing my notes on how the JavaScript compiler maps scopes and identifiers before runtime. It covers topics like Hoisting, the Temporal Dead Zone (TDZ), and the exact differences between let and var.

This article is inspired by Kyle Simpson's Deep JavaScript Foundations workshop on FrontendMasters, which provided the groundwork for this
study. Thanks for sharing your knowledge and contributing so much to the developer community.
